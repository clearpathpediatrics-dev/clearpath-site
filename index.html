FAQs

Questions, answered.

Everything you need to know about ClearPath Pediatrics, our services, and how we support your family.

ClearPath Pediatrics is a private, RN-led care navigation and education service designed to support families with children who have complex or chronic medical needs. We help parents understand medical information, stay organized, and feel confident managing their child's care between clinical appointments.

No. ClearPath Pediatrics is not a medical practice and does not provide clinical care, diagnosis, or treatment. We are a care navigation and education service. All clinical decisions remain with your child's licensed healthcare providers.

ClearPath RNs are licensed Registered Nurses with experience in pediatric care. They are knowledgeable, compassionate, and dedicated to helping families navigate the complexities of pediatric healthcare — but they do not practice medicine or provide clinical recommendations.

ClearPath Pediatrics is currently based in Phoenix, Arizona. Our services are delivered virtually, which means we can support families across the country. Please reach out to confirm availability in your specific location.

We offer three core options: (1) Care Binder Setup — a one-time service to organize your child's full medical documentation; (2) Essentials Membership — monthly RN check-ins, a personalized care plan, and secure messaging; (3) Guided Care Membership — two monthly RN sessions, priority messaging, and comprehensive coordination support.

Our Care Binder Setup is a one-time fee of $97. The Essentials Membership is $149/month, and the Guided Care Membership is $249/month. There are no hidden fees. You can cancel your membership at any time.

ClearPath Pediatrics is a private-pay service and does not accept insurance at this time. We do not bill insurance companies, and our services are not covered by most insurance plans. Some families use HSA/FSA funds — please check with your plan administrator to confirm eligibility.

Many families find that ClearPath services may qualify as an eligible expense under HSA/FSA plans. However, eligibility varies by plan. We recommend checking with your HSA/FSA administrator to confirm whether our services qualify.

The first step is scheduling a free 30-minute consultation. During this call, we'll learn about your child's situation, answer your questions, and help you decide whether ClearPath is the right fit. There's no pressure and no obligation.

We'll talk about your child's diagnosis, current providers, what's feeling most overwhelming, and what kind of support would be most helpful. By the end of the call, you'll have a clear sense of whether ClearPath can help and which service option makes the most sense for your family.

If you decide to move forward after your consultation, we can typically onboard you within a few business days. Your RN will reach out to schedule your first formal session and begin building your care navigation plan.

Check-ins are conducted via phone or secure video. During each session, your RN will review any recent changes in your child's care, help you prepare for upcoming appointments, address any questions or concerns since the last session, and update your care navigation plan as needed.

Secure messaging allows you to send non-urgent questions and updates to your ClearPath RN between scheduled sessions. Your RN will respond within 1–2 business days. This is not for emergencies — if you have a medical emergency, please call 911 or go to your nearest emergency room.

ClearPath RNs do not physically attend appointments. However, we do extensive appointment preparation with families before visits and help you debrief and process the information afterward — so you walk in prepared and leave with clarity.

ClearPath does not communicate directly with your child's medical team on your behalf. Our role is to help you understand, organize, and prepare — so that you can communicate more effectively with your providers yourself.

We take privacy seriously. ClearPath uses secure, HIPAA-compliant communication tools for all client interactions. We do not share your family's information with third parties without your explicit consent.

ClearPath is not an emergency service. If your child is experiencing a medical emergency, call 911 immediately or go to your nearest emergency room. Do not wait to contact ClearPath — your child's safety comes first.

Yes. ClearPath memberships are month-to-month with no long-term commitment required. You can cancel at any time by contacting us. We'll confirm your cancellation and ensure a smooth transition.
