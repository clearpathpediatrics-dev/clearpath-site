How to Organize Your Child's Pediatric Prior Authorization Requests: A Parent's Guide

July 20, 2026 · 4 min read · ClearPath Pediatrics

pediatric prior authorizationpediatric care navigationinsurance approvals for kidsorganizing medical paperworkpediatric care coordination

If you've ever waited on hold to find out whether your child's medication, imaging, or specialist visit was approved, you already know how confusing prior authorizations can be. A prior authorization (often shortened to "prior auth" or "PA") is your insurance company's process for approving certain services before they happen. When you're juggling multiple providers and deadlines, it's easy for one request to get lost — and that delay can be stressful when your child needs care.

The good news: a simple tracking system can turn a scattered, anxiety-filled process into something you actually feel on top of. This guide walks you through how to organize prior authorization requests so you always know where each one stands.

Why Prior Authorizations Deserve Their Own Tracking System

Prior authorizations move between several hands — your child's provider's office submits the request, the insurance company reviews it, and sometimes a specialty pharmacy or imaging center is involved too. Each handoff is a place where things can stall. Without a central record, you may not know whether a request was even submitted, let alone approved or denied.

Keeping your own log doesn't replace the work your provider's office does. It simply gives you a clear picture so you can follow up at the right moment, ask informed questions, and avoid the frustration of a canceled appointment or an unfilled prescription.

What Information Should You Track for Each Prior Authorization?

For every request, jot down a few key details in one place — a notebook, a spreadsheet, or a note on your phone all work. Try to capture:

  • The service or medication being requested, exactly as your provider named it
  • The date submitted and which office or provider submitted it
  • A reference or authorization number if one is given
  • The status — pending, approved, denied, or needs more information
  • The expiration date of the authorization, since many are time-limited
  • A contact name and phone number for follow-up questions

Update the status each time you learn something new. That single habit keeps you from repeating calls or losing track of where a request lives.

How Long Does a Pediatric Prior Authorization Take?

Timelines vary widely depending on your insurance plan and the type of service. Some routine requests are processed within a few business days, while more complex ones can take a week or more. Urgent or expedited reviews may move faster, and your provider's office can note when a request is time-sensitive. Because there's no single answer, the most useful thing you can do is write down the submission date and ask, "When should I expect to hear back?" That way you know when it's reasonable to follow up rather than guessing.

When and How Should You Follow Up?

A good rule of thumb: if you haven't heard anything by the date you were told to expect an answer, that's your cue to check in. Start with the office that submitted the request, since they can see the status on their end. You can also call the member services number on the back of your insurance card and reference your tracking notes.

Keep your tone calm and your questions specific: "Can you tell me the status of the prior authorization submitted on [date] for [service]?" Having your reference number ready makes these calls faster. Note the date, who you spoke with, and what they told you — that running record becomes invaluable if a request is delayed or denied.

Turning a Denial Into a Next Step

A denial isn't always the end of the road, and it often comes down to missing information or a paperwork detail rather than the care itself. If a request is denied, ask what the specific reason was and what the next steps are. Your child's provider can help determine whether to submit additional documentation. Keeping your organized notes handy means you can act quickly instead of starting from scratch.
